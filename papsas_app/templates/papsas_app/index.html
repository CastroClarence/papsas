Welcome to the Philippines Association of Practitioners of Student Affairs and Services, Inc.

The Philippine Association of Practitioners of Student Affairs and Services, Inc. is the professional organization of student affairs and services administrator and practitioners in the Philippines, dedicated to the pursuit of excellence in student affairs and services work. It is an effective channel through which student affairs practitioners can develop and enhance their capabilities.

PAPSAS, Inc. is committed to the formation of the Filipino educators through the conduct of effective and relevant programs and services addressing student issues and student affairs development. PAPSAS, Inc. is dedicated in building the competencies of its members through programs and services it offers.

𝗣𝗔𝗣𝗦𝗔𝗦 𝗥𝗲𝗴𝗶𝗼𝗻 𝟱 𝗖𝗵𝗮𝗽𝘁𝗲𝗿 𝗹𝗮𝘂𝗻𝗰𝗵𝗲𝘀 𝗕𝗶𝗰𝗼𝗹 𝗬𝗼𝘂𝗻𝗴 𝗟𝗲𝗮𝗱𝗲𝗿𝘀 𝗦𝘂𝗺𝗺𝗶𝘁

The Region 5 Chapter of the Philippine Association of Practitioners of Student Affairs and Services, Inc. (PAPSAS) launched the Bicol Young Leaders Summit (BYLS) as its flagship student development program.

The 1st PAPSAS BYLS was hosted by the Ateneo de Naga University, Naga City, on January 20 – 21, 2023. More than 200 student leaders, student affairs and services (SAS) administrators and staff, and advisers of student councils and organizations from Bicol Region participated in this event.

The summit featured three resource speakers, namely: Ralph Jacinto Quiblat, director of the Ateneo de Manila University Office of Student Activities; Magno Edilberto Conag III, founder and CEO of Nueca Technologies, Inc.; and Ben Secretario, president of the Ten Outstanding Boy Scouts of the Philippines Association. Consistent with the BYLS’s whole-person formation framework, the said speakers discussed the four-session topics: Self Awareness, Servant Leadership, Project Development, and Collaboration and Network Building.

Naga City Mayor Nelson Legacion and ADNU University President Fr. Roberto Exequiel Rivera, S.J. graced the occasion and delivered their keynote speeches.

Aside from attending the plenary talks and conversations, the participants took part in The Amazing Race-inspired game, where groups had to complete tasks in eight stations. After the teambuilding challenges, the same groups each performed in the talent show dubbed as “BYLS Got Talent.”

Group representatives also presented their project ideas after undergoing a project development workshop using the Business Model Canvas template.

A synthesis given by the master of ceremony Allysa Salvino, and a commitment-building pledge led by BYLS 2023 project head and ADNU student leader-debater Jerwin Roy Villacruz capped off the two-day event.

The 1st BYLS was organized by a team of students, with the supervision of PAPSAS Region 5 Chapter headed by its Regional President Rodolfo ‘Sonny’ Virtus Jr.
